🏠 1. Open Property Checklist

Purpose: Prepare a property for owner arrival or occupancy.

Steps:
	1.	Exterior
	•	Unlock gates and entry doors
	•	Remove security devices (covers, locks, barriers)
	•	Turn on exterior lighting and landscape systems
	•	Inspect driveway, paths, and pool area for debris
	2.	Utilities & Systems
	•	Turn on main water supply
	•	Turn on water heater and confirm power source
	•	Turn on electricity breakers (note any tripped circuits)
	•	Turn on HVAC and set thermostat to owner preference
	3.	Interior Setup
	•	Open blinds/curtains for natural light
	•	Test all lights, ceiling fans, and switches
	•	Check refrigerator, freezer, and appliances
	•	Run faucets/toilets briefly to flush standing water
	4.	Safety Check
	•	Test smoke and CO detectors
	•	Inspect fire extinguishers and expiration dates
	•	Ensure security alarm system is functional
	5.	Final Prep
	•	Place welcome note or owner supplies
	•	Report any issues or needed repairs
	•	Lock up and confirm alarm re-armed upon exit

🔒 2. Close Property Checklist

Purpose: Secure and prepare property for vacancy.

Steps:
	1.	Utilities & Systems
	•	Turn off main water supply
	•	Drain water lines if required
	•	Set water heater to vacation/off mode
	•	Adjust HVAC to energy-efficient temperature
	•	Unplug non-essential appliances
	2.	Interior
	•	Close blinds/curtains as directed
	•	Empty refrigerator/freezer if long-term vacancy
	•	Confirm all doors/windows locked
	•	Remove perishable items and trash
	3.	Exterior
	•	Store outdoor furniture, cushions, umbrellas
	•	Turn off irrigation if instructed
	•	Secure gates and garage doors
	•	Cover or secure pool and spa
	4.	Security
	•	Arm alarm and confirm monitoring status
	•	Leave key and access instructions securely stored
	5.	Documentation
	•	Take final photos of each room and exterior
	•	Log readings for electricity, water, or gas
	•	Submit closing report

⸻

🔍 3. Property Inspection Checklist

Purpose: Routine check for maintenance, security, and system health.

Steps:
	1.	Exterior
	•	Inspect roof, gutters, and downspouts
	•	Check for storm damage or debris
	•	Examine pool/spa condition
	•	Look for pests, rodents, or animal activity
	2.	Interior
	•	Check for leaks, mold, or water stains
	•	Test all lights and ceiling fans
	•	Ensure HVAC functioning properly
	•	Flush toilets and run faucets to prevent stagnation
	•	Verify windows/doors are locked and sealed
	3.	Appliances & Systems
	•	Run dishwasher, washing machine (short cycle) monthly
	•	Confirm refrigerator/freezer temperature
	•	Check smoke/CO detectors and batteries
	•	Test security system or cameras
	4.	Documentation
	•	Take time-stamped photos of key areas
	•	Note maintenance needs or issues
	•	Send report to client with recommendations

⸻

🧹 4. Storm Preparation Checklist

Purpose: Ready the property for hurricanes or severe weather.

Steps:
	1.	Exterior
	•	Bring in outdoor furniture, planters, décor
	•	Secure loose items (grill, hoses, etc.)
	•	Lower or secure hurricane shutters
	•	Check generator fuel and operation
	2.	Interior
	•	Unplug electronics and sensitive devices
	•	Move valuables and paperwork to safe location
	•	Close interior doors to reduce pressure shifts
	3.	Systems
	•	Turn off main water supply if necessary
	•	Set HVAC to safe level
	•	Backup alarm system power (if available)
	4.	Final
	•	Document pre-storm condition with photos
	•	Notify client when prep is complete

⸻

🧾 5. Post-Storm Inspection Checklist

Purpose: Evaluate and document condition after severe weather.

Steps:
	1.	Exterior
	•	Inspect roof, siding, and gutters for damage
	•	Check pool and yard for debris
	•	Verify windows, shutters, and doors for impact damage
	•	Confirm fencing and gates intact
	2.	Interior
	•	Check for leaks, water intrusion, or dampness
	•	Inspect ceilings and floors for stains or swelling
	•	Confirm power restored and systems operational
	3.	Documentation
	•	Take photos before cleanup
	•	Record any damage or insurance-related details
	•	Send client report with next steps

⸻

🧰 6. Maintenance Follow-Up Checklist

Purpose: Manage ongoing or recurring service tasks.

Steps:
	1.	Scheduling
	•	Confirm date/time with vendor
	•	Verify access details and lockbox codes
	•	Notify client if they requested updates
	2.	On-Site Verification
	•	Meet vendor if required
	•	Observe completion of work
	•	Confirm cleanup and restore access
	3.	Post-Service
	•	Take photos of completed work
	•	Collect and file invoices
	•	Mark maintenance log as complete
